INGLESE POTENZIATO

During colonialism, London became the pulsing heart and the financial, political, and administrative center of the British Empire, controlling global maritime trade. The city expanded culturally, with institutions like the British Museum accumulating world treasures, while colonial goods like tea and furniture transformed the everyday life and status symbols of the lower classes. Concurrently, London became a refuge for intellectuals and future global leaders, turning into the birthplace of Pan-African, movements that eventually contributed to the dismantling of the empire itself.
